# UG Rules & Regulations — Curriculum, Credits, and Registration

Source: IIT Bombay Academic Office — Rules & Regulations for Undergraduate Programmes (B.Tech., B.S., B.Des., Dual Degree students admitted from AY 2007-2008), Updated June 2025.
URL: https://acad.iitb.ac.in/sites/default/files/UG_RULE_BOOK.pdf

## Introduction

IIT Bombay follows a credit-based semester system. There are two regular semesters in a year: the Autumn Semester (July-Nov, also called Semester 1) and the Spring Semester (Jan-April/May, also called Semester 2). There is also a Summer Term (May-June) during which a few courses are offered.

The B.Tech./Dual Degree/B.S. programmes consist of courses in basic sciences, humanities and social sciences, engineering and technology, and other related topics, broadly organized in three phases: an intense study of sciences/mathematics/humanities, followed by engineering sciences and technical arts, followed by subjects in the student's chosen area of study.

Academic matters are governed by the Senate, which is the statutory and supreme body for all academic matters. The Dean of Academic Programmes (Dean, AP) and Associate Dean (AP) convene the Undergraduate Programmes Committee (UGPC) and Postgraduate Programmes Committee (PGPC). Performance and evaluation issues are monitored by the Undergraduate Academic Performance Evaluation Committee (UGAPEC) and Postgraduate Academic Performance Evaluation Committee (PGAPEC). At the department level, the Department Undergraduate Committee (DUGC) and Department Postgraduate Committee (DPGC) handle academic matters. Each student is assigned a Faculty Adviser on joining the Institute.

## Course Credit Structure

Credit (C) for a course is obtained by multiplying the number of hours of instruction per week by two (2) for lecture and tutorial hours, and by one (1) for laboratory hours, i.e., C = 2L + 2T + P. For example, a theory course with L=2, T=1, P=0 carries 6 credits; a laboratory course with L=0, T=1, P=3 carries 5 credits.

Seminars typically carry 3 credits. B.Tech. Projects (BTP-I and BTP-II) may be offered as a single 12-credit unit or split into two 6-credit stages. A Dual Degree Project (DDP) carries a total of 72 credits, spread over fourteen months starting after the eighth semester. One of NCC/NSO/NSS is a mandatory non-credit requirement, graded Pass (PP)/Not Pass (NP).

Minimum credits required for the award of a B.Tech./B.S./B.Des. degree are between 266 and 282 (till the 2021 batch), nominally divided into Basic Sciences (60-62), Engineering Sciences & Skills (30), HSS Electives (12), Institute Electives (12), and Departmental courses (152-168).

## Minor and Honours

A Minor requires 30 credits of additional courses in a discipline other than the student's major; allotment is based on highest CPI during pre-registration. Honours requires 24 additional credits in the student's own discipline. Students can pursue more than one minor, or an Honours plus a Minor, subject to timetable feasibility and discussion with their Faculty Adviser. If 30 credits are not completed, the Minor is not awarded, though the individual course credits still appear on the transcript.

## Registration

Registration at the start of each semester, per the dates in the Academic Calendar, is mandatory for every student. Students who do not register in a semester without prior UGAPEC permission are liable to have their studentship cancelled. Students cannot re-register for courses they have already passed, and registration requires Faculty Adviser approval. Students with outstanding dues to the Institute or a hostel are not permitted to register.

Late registration is permitted only for valid reasons, till the date specified in the Academic Calendar, on payment of a late registration fee.

An Institute Elective course must not be from the student's own Academic Unit and must not be a Core or Department Elective course in the student's programme.

Course Adjustment (addition/deletion of courses) is permitted on or before a date typically one week after semester commencement, set in the Academic Calendar, and must be done in consultation with the Faculty Adviser. Dropping of courses (with a 'W' grade, no impact on SPI/CPI) is permitted until typically 20-30 days after the end of the mid-semester examination, provided the minimum credit requirement of 18 credits per semester is maintained.

A minimum of 5 students must be registered for a course to run as a Department/Institute Elective; even a single student registered for a Minor course is sufficient for it to be offered.

## Academic Standing (Categories)

Depending on overall academic performance (especially the two preceding regular semesters), students are placed into one of six categories:

- **Category I**: CPI at least 8, no outstanding FR/DX/DR/W grade in a core course.
- **Category II**: CPI less than 8, no outstanding FR/DX/DR/W grade in a core course.
- **Category III**: At least one outstanding FR/DX/DR/W in core courses and at most one FR or DX grade in any other course in the two preceding regular semesters, having earned at least 18 credits in each.
- **Category IV**: At least one outstanding FR/DX/DR/W in core courses and more than one FR or DX grade in any other course in the two preceding regular semesters, having earned at least 18 credits in each.
- **Category V**: A student who has not earned at least 18 credits in at least one of the previous two regular registered semesters.
- **Category VI (ARP - Academic Rehabilitation Programme)**: Students who have accumulated FR/DX grades worth 36 credits in core courses. Category VI supersedes all other categories.

### Permissible Registration Load by Category

| Academic Standing | Maximum Credits Allowed |
|---|---|
| Category I | 54 credits |
| Category II, III, IV | 48 credits |
| Category V | 36 credits |
| Category VI (ARP) | 24 credits |

Every student must register for a minimum of 18 credits each semester (unless most minimum credit requirements are already completed). The Faculty Adviser is the competent authority to approve course registration in all cases, and may prescribe lower credits than the maximum, especially for Categories III, IV, V and VI.

## Audit Courses, Guided Study, and Attendance

A student can register for a course in 'Audit' mode (graded 'AU', zero grade point, not included in SPI/CPI) with prior permission of the instructor. A UG student can audit a maximum of TWO courses for the entire duration of the programme, and only students in Category I and II may audit a course during a regular semester.

Guided Study (self-study mode outside the curriculum) is available to Category I students, up to one course per semester from the third semester onward, subject to a maximum of four courses in the entire programme, and requires DUGC recommendation and UGAPEC approval.

Attendance in classes is mandatory from the very beginning of the semester. Students who miss even a single lecture among the first three lectures of a course are liable to be deregistered from that course. IIT Bombay expects 100% attendance from its students.
