# UG Rules & Regulations — Examination, Grading, and Performance Requirements

Source: IIT Bombay Academic Office — Rules & Regulations for Undergraduate Programmes (B.Tech., B.S., B.Des., Dual Degree students admitted from AY 2007-2008), Updated June 2025.
URL: https://acad.iitb.ac.in/sites/default/files/UG_RULE_BOOK.pdf

## Modes of Evaluation

For theory courses, assessment modes include quizzes, class tests, home assignments, group assignments, viva-voce, mid-semester and semester-end examinations. In-semester evaluation typically carries 50-60% weightage, including one mid-semester exam of two hours (about 25-30% weightage) held per the Academic Calendar. The instructor may set aside up to 20% of in-semester marks for attendance/class participation. The semester-end examination typically carries 40-50% weightage, is of about 3 hours duration, covers the full syllabus, and is mandatory.

For laboratory courses, in-semester work typically carries 75% and the semester-end test 25% weightage. It is obligatory to maintain a laboratory journal. The final examination for lab courses is typically held a week before final theory examinations.

Seminars are evaluated based on a written report and an oral presentation before a panel of examiners appointed by the DUGC/coordinator.

## Grading System

IIT Bombay follows a letter grade system with associated Grade Points:

| Letter Grade | Grade Point | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| AP | 10 | Exceptional performance (awarded only in courses with >50 registered students, max 2% of class) |
| AA | 10 | |
| AB | 9 | |
| BB | 8 | |
| BC | 7 | |
| CC | 6 | |
| CD | 5 | |
| DD | 4 | Minimum passing grade |
| FR | 0 | Fail and repeat the course/equivalent course |
| FF | 0 | Fail & Re-examination allowed (max grade DD after re-exam) |
| DX | — | Drop due to inadequate attendance (below 80%); credits not included in CPI |
| W | — | Course Withdrawn |
| AU | — | Satisfactory in an Audit course |
| PP | — | Passed (NCC/NSO/NSS) |
| NP | — | Not Passed (NCC/NSO/NSS) |
| II | — | Incomplete (placeholder, converted after re-examination) |
| DR | — | Dropped (placeholder; course must be cleared in a subsequent semester) |

A student passes a course with any grade from "AP" to "DD" (or "AU" for an audit course), and fails with grade "FR" and/or "DX". "FF", "II" and "DR" are placeholder grades.

An "FR" grade is awarded when the instructor (or examiner panel, for projects) judges performance below an acceptable threshold, or for academic malpractice. A "DX" grade is awarded for inadequate attendance: if a student's attendance falls below 80% of total expected attendance (counted from the first contact hour), the instructor may award DX, which is treated as equivalent to a course drop for CPI purposes. No concession is given for medical grounds when calculating attendance for DX, unless the instructor declared in the first week that no DX would be awarded for that course.

For first-year UG students, no direct 'FR' grade is awarded after the end-semester exam on the first attempt — the fail grade is 'FF' at first instance, and the student reappears in a re-examination, after which a DD or FR grade is allotted.

## Semester Performance Index (SPI) and Cumulative Performance Index (CPI)

SPI is the weighted average of grade points obtained in all courses registered by the student during a semester, weighted by course credits: SPI = (C1g1 + C2g2 + ... + Cn*gn) / (C1 + C2 + ... + Cn), calculated to two decimal places. A failed course (FR) contributes zero grade points to that semester's SPI.

CPI is an up-to-date assessment of overall performance since the student entered the Institute, calculated similarly across all courses counted toward the degree's minimum requirements, to two decimal places. If a course was failed and later cleared, the CPI reflects only the new passing grade, not the earlier fail. The transcript reflects two CPI values: CPI (Courses only) and CPI (Courses + Project).

Courses beyond the minimum degree requirements (e.g., additional electives) are shown separately on the transcript and do not affect the basic CPI calculation.

## Transcript

The transcript is a consolidated statement of a student's academic performance for all semesters since joining, given on successful completion of the programme along with the degree certificate. Every course registered and grade obtained (including FR, DX, W) is recorded, for courses registered from Autumn Semester 2016-17 onwards. Additional courses, and any Minor/Honours earned, are shown separately. Semester Grade Cards were discontinued from Spring Semester 2014-15; students view grades via ASC (Academic Section Cell portal) as soon as instructors upload them.

## Answer Scripts and Compensatory Time

Evaluated answer scripts are preserved by the Instructor/Departmental Office for one semester. PwD students are entitled to compensatory time during written examinations — not less than 20 minutes per hour of examination for those using a scribe/reader, or a minimum additional hour for a 3-hour exam if not using a scribe (with time in the examination duration prorated for shorter exams, minimum 5-minute increments).

## Performance Requirements for Award of Degree

To be conferred a degree, a student must: pass all prescribed courses under general institutional and departmental requirements; satisfactorily fulfil other academic requirements such as practical training, NSS/NSO/NCC, work visits, seminar and projects; have paid all Institute dues; and have no pending case of indiscipline.

Students with poor academic performance — specifically, FR/DX grades accumulated for at least 36 credits in core courses — are transferred to the **Academic Rehabilitation Programme (ARP)**, where a dedicated ARP Faculty Adviser assists manual registration for a maximum of 24 credits. A student who reduces total FR/DX credits below 36 returns to being a regular student; failure to exit ARP after three consecutive semesters may lead to a recommendation for termination of registration.

### Exit Degree Option

Students in B.Tech., B.S., B.Des., or B.Tech.+M.Tech. (DD) programmes are eligible for an exit degree after completing minimum 160 credits (including ES 200 and HS 200) and at least 6 semesters (application possible after the 4th semester). Exit from B.Tech./Dual Degree maps to "Bachelor of Science (3-year) in Engineering" (with or without specialization); exit from B.S. in Chemistry/Mathematics maps to B.Sc. (3 years); exit from B.S. in Economics maps to B.A. (3 years); exit from B.Des. maps to B.Des. (3 years).

### NPTEL/SWAYAM and Semester Exchange

Students may register for NPTEL/SWAYAM courses in lieu of departmental/institute electives, up to 12 credits normally (24 credits for ARP students or those in extension years, with DUGC approval). A 12-week NPTEL/SWAYAM course is typically equivalent to 6 IITB credits (up to 8 in special cases); an 8-week course is equivalent to 3 or 4 credits, depending on DUGC/DPGC approval. Grades from such courses are not included in SPI/CPI calculation but are recorded on the transcript as-is.

## Change of Branch and LASE Conversion

Branch Change into JoSAA-administered branches was eliminated by the 256th Senate meeting (applicable from JEE Advanced 2023 admits onward). Conversion to the Liberal Arts, Sciences and Engineering (LASE) programme via Branch Change remains available. Eligibility requires: completion of prescribed first-year course credits, no FR/DX/DR/W grades in the first two semesters, and an "eligibility-CPI" of at least 7.0 based on specified first-year courses. A valid branch-change request needs either a "Branch-Change-CPI" of at least 9 with a seat available, or a seat available in the target branch without the source branch's strength falling more than 25% below its sanctioned strength. Branch change can happen only once, at the start of the second academic year, and decisions are final.
