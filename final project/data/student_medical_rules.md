# IITB Medical Entitlement Rules for Registered Students

Source: IIT Bombay Academic Office — Student Medical Rules
URL: https://acad.iitb.ac.in/sites/default/files/student-medical-rules.pdf

## Eligibility

All undergraduate (UG) and postgraduate (PG) students registered for the Autumn (July-December) and Spring (January-June) semesters are entitled to treatment at the IITB Hospital while present on campus. UG and PG students who graduate mid-semester cease to have medical entitlement five days after graduation or the last date of vacating the hostel, as informed by the Associate Dean (Student Affairs).

UG students with academic extensions (due to medical dropout or poor academic performance) remain eligible for medical treatment for an additional 2 semesters, only if staying on campus. PG students are entitled to an additional 1 semester only.

PhD scholars entitled to medical benefits can avail treatment at IITB Hospital throughout the year for a maximum of 6 years, or till ten days after the PhD viva-voce, whichever is earlier, if staying on campus.

Spouse and children of entitled PhD students, and of QIP students, can avail IITB Hospital outpatient facility (QIP dependents get OPD and IPD, but no reimbursement). Post-doctoral scholars can avail only the outpatient facility. Government-employed/company-sponsored students must follow GOI/company-specific rules.

## Outstation Medical Benefits

Presently, no outstation medical treatment is admissible to students except accidental treatment.

## Referral and Second Opinion

IITB Hospital is the primary health care centre. When secondary or tertiary treatment is needed, students are referred to one of the empanelled hospitals. On IITB Hospital's recommendation, a student may be referred to an empanelled hospital for a second opinion when diagnosis or treatment is not possible on campus.

## Reimbursement

A student is registered at IITB Hospital only after a medical file is issued (following verification of the vaccination certificate). Eligible reimbursements include:

- Cost of medicines purchased from the market and pathology/radiology charges incurred at an empanelled facility on the advice of the IITB Hospital Medical Officer (pre-exposure vaccines are not reimbursable).
- Expenditure for indoor treatment (on IITB Hospital's advice) at a recognised hospital in economy/general class accommodation, subject to a ceiling of Rs. 2.0 lakhs per semester. If the student opts for a higher class, the cost difference must be borne by the student. Emergency surgeries/procedures following transfer from IITB Hospital are reimbursed under the same ceiling; elective surgeries and procedures are not reimbursed.

There is no reimbursement for treatments related to dentistry, infertility, maternity, cosmetology, substance abuse, obesity, joint replacement, or organ transplant. Travel costs for treatment are not reimbursable.

## Settlement of Medical Claims

Medical claims must be submitted for settlement against a medical advance within one month of discharge. OPD bills and in-patient bills (when no advance is taken) should be submitted within six months, to the medical unit of the administration section. Processing takes up to approximately 45 days from the date of receipt.

## Prolonged Treatment

Treatment of chronic cases or prolonged illnesses (defined as requiring treatment for over 30 days) is the responsibility of the parents/guardian. Medical certificates from the treating doctor are required for resuming academic registration.

## Accidental Injury Reimbursement

All registered students are covered under accidental injury insurance: treatment cost of hospitalisation in the general ward category is covered (pre/post-admission OPD treatment is not covered), with maximum reimbursement of 2 lakhs per incident. Treatment can be availed anywhere in India, provided it is at the same station where the injury occurred. Elective surgery is not reimbursable.

## Medical Advance

Students are eligible for a medical advance up to Rs. 1 lakh when referred for in-patient treatment at an empanelled hospital. Application is via email to the in-charge CMO (medicaladvance@iitb.ac.in) with the IITB Hospital reference, student ID, and the treating hospital's estimate attached. On CMO recommendation, the request is forwarded to the Dean of Student Affairs for approval and payment.

Treatment at a non-empanelled hospital is not eligible for a medical advance.

## Certificates

Students registered at IITB Hospital (with a medical file) are eligible for: (1) medical certificate for treatment and fitness, (2) swimming fitness certificate for the IIT Bombay swimming pool, and (3) vaccination certificate for vaccines administered at IITB Hospital. Students are not eligible for authentication of treatment certificates issued by outside doctors.

## Additional Points

The Dean (Student Affairs) is authorised to recommend additional reimbursement of up to INR 5 lakhs for an eligible medical claim, and holds additional financial powers for medical emergencies on a case-by-case basis; such amounts are treated as a loan the student must settle before obtaining their degree.
