# Rules for Award of Medals and Academic Prizes for UG & PG

Source: IIT Bombay Academic Office (Approved in the 260th Senate, 14 Feb 2024; Updated 9 March 2026)
URL: https://acad.iitb.ac.in/sites/default/files/RulesforAwardofMedalsandAcademicprizesforUGandPG.pdf

## President of India Medal (PGM)

Awarded to the B.S./B.Tech. (with honours) or B.Tech.+M.Tech. Dual Degree/IDDDP student (excluding those without honours) with the highest CPI at the end of the 4th year, based only on mandatory credit requirements, calculated after final retagging of courses. For IDDDP, the medal is given in the student's parent department. A student is considered for the medal if their overall CPI (including honours) is 9.0 or above. In case of a tie with the Institute Gold Medal, CPI across all courses (including additional and minor courses) up to the end of the 4th year is used to break the tie. An "II" and/or "W" grade is not a bar.

**Disqualification for medals** applies for: award of "DX", "FF", "FR", or "NP" grade(s) in any course; extension for final-stage MTPs; completing the programme beyond its prescribed duration; or any punishment under DAC/ADAC during the programme. Students taking NPTEL/SWAYAM/Semester exchange courses remain eligible for medals.

## Institute Gold Medal (IGM)

Awarded to the most outstanding student of the Dual Degree/IDDDP programme (or B.Tech./B.S. with honours) in the year the other category's student wins the PGM — i.e., PGM and IGM alternate between the two tracks based on which produces the top overall performer. The Institute Gold Medal has been discontinued from the 2022-23 admission batch onward (students admitted till 2021-22 are still awarded it). Eligibility, tie-breaking, and disqualification rules mirror the PGM.

## Institute Silver Medal (ISM)

Awarded in each academic unit across programmes including B.Tech. (with honours), Dual Degree, B.S. (with honours), B.Des., M.Sc., M.Tech., MPP, M.Des., MBA, MS by Research, MA by Research, and LASE, to the top-qualifying student meeting all credit and honours requirements. IDDDP students (excluding those without honours) are considered in their 5th year, with the medal awarded in the department of the higher degree. If the top-ranked student in a unit wins PGM or IGM, the 2nd-ranked student receives the department Silver Medal (from the 56th and 58th Convocation respectively).

Eligibility requires overall CPI of 9.0 or above (or CPI+honours CPI of 9.0+ for B.Tech./B.S., or overall CPI including honours 9.0+ for Dual Degree/IDDDP). Institute Silver Medals for Dual Degree/IDDDP/Masters programmes are awarded where the graduating cohort in a department-programme is 30 or more; smaller cohorts are clubbed across departments per specified pairing rules (e.g., MPP with M.Tech., IDDDP with Dual Degree, MS by Research across all departments). Exit degrees (MS by research/M.Phil exit from PhD, PGDIIT exit from M.Tech., BSc Engg exit from B.Tech., etc.) are not considered for medals. No Institute medals are awarded to M.Eng/MDP/EMBA students.

## Dr. Shankar Dayal Sharma Gold Medal

Awarded to the most outstanding student in terms of general proficiency — combining academic excellence, extracurricular activities, and social service — among all graduating students, based on overall CPI. Same disqualification rules apply as for the PGM/IGM.

## Donor's Medals

Several named medals are awarded based on specific criteria set by their donors, including:
- **Miss Jayati Deshmukh Memorial Gold Medal** — awarded to the Institute Silver Medal recipient for B.Tech. in Computer Science & Engineering.
- **Vidyasagar Nehra Gold Medal** and **Prof Madhav Kulkarni Lt.Col.(R) Gold Medal** — both awarded to the Institute Silver Medal recipient for B.Tech. in Civil Engineering.
- **Hindi Vidya Bhavan Gold Medal** — awarded to the highest-CPI student in the SJMSOM MBA programme.
- **Sharad Maloo Gold Medal** — awarded to the second-highest scorer (after the Dr. Shankar Dayal Sharma Gold Medal winner) from B.Tech./Dual Degree programmes.
- **Abhijeet Banerjee SJMSOM Silver Medal** — awarded to the 2nd-highest CPI student in the SJMSOM MBA programme (from the 60th Convocation).

## Academic Prizes for On-Roll Students

Academic prizes, based on the weighted SPI average of the previous academic year's performance, are given to on-roll students of UG, M.Sc., M.Tech., M.Des., M.Phil, MBA, MA by Research, MS by Research, and MPP programmes:

- **2nd year UG students**: top 20 by weighted SPI average — 1st prize Rs. 3000, 2nd prize Rs. 2000, plus an additional prize of Rs. 2000.
- **3rd/4th year UG, 2nd-4th year B.Des., 5th year Dual Degree**: top 2 by weighted SPI average — both 1st (Rs. 3000) and 2nd (Rs. 2000) prizes if class strength is 25 or above; only 1st prize if class strength is below 25.
- **2nd year M.Sc., M.Tech., M.Des., M.Phil, MBA, MA by Research, MS by Research, MPP**: same top-2 structure as above.

The weighted SPI average formula is: (Sem 1 Credits × Sem 1 SPI + Sem 2 Credits × Sem 2 SPI) / (Sem 1 Credits + Sem 2 Credits). A student must have a weighted SPI average of 9 or above to be eligible, and the academic unit must have a class strength of 5 or more. Where a department has multiple specializations, students across all specializations are clubbed together for the purpose of Academic Prize consideration.
